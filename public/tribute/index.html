// 02 — THE LEGACY

From a single Wapiti
to a multi-domain force.

Born on 8 October 1932 with a handful of biplanes and six officers, the Indian Air Force has grown into the world's fourth-largest air arm — a doctrine-driven, technology-led service safeguarding 3.28 million square kilometres of sovereign airspace.

  • 1932

    Constituted as the Royal Indian Air Force with No. 1 Squadron flying Westland Wapitis from Drigh Road.

  • 1950

    Republic of India drops the "Royal" prefix. Vampire jets usher in the jet age.

  • 1971

    Decisive air superiority over East Pakistan accelerates the liberation of Bangladesh in 13 days.

  • 2020+

    Rafale, Tejas Mk1A, S-400 and AMCA programmes anchor the leap to fifth-generation air power.
